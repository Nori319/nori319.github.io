PageSentinel Privacy Policy

Enacted: June 9, 2025

Last Updated: September 25, 2025

This Privacy Policy (hereinafter “Policy”) sets forth how the individual developer (the “Developer”) of the smartphone app PageSentinel (the “App”) handles user data with respect to privacy protection. By using the App, you agree to this Policy.

Article 1 (Information Collected)

  1. User Input Data
    URLs to be monitored, reload intervals, detection keywords, CSS selectors, and other information entered or configured in the App.
  2. In-App Settings
    User-selected display and behavior settings such as Anti-Flicker level, scroll delays, step waits, and actions on detection.
  3. Device Metadata
    OS version, device type (iPhone/iPad), app version, and other technical information necessary for operation and improvement. No UUIDs or personally identifying information are collected.
  4. Support Inquiries (Optional)
    Content you provide when contacting support by email (e.g., bug reports or requests). This may include settings or device information you choose to share.

Article 2 (Purpose of Use)

  • Monitoring and reloading of user-specified web pages and related automation
  • Control of automatic reloads and anti-flicker behavior
  • Saving in-app settings and restoring them after relaunch
  • Analysis of anonymized technical data for bug fixes and feature improvements
  • Customer support (only when you contact us)

Article 3 (Prohibition of Third-Party Provision)

The Developer will not provide the information described in Article 1 to third parties without your consent, except where required by law or when you explicitly consent in the course of support communications.

Article 4 (Cookies and Web Technologies)

The App uses WebView to display web pages. Use of cookies, localStorage, and similar web data is governed by the visited websites’ policies. The App does not independently collect or store such website data.

Article 5 (Data Retention)

  • User input and settings are stored locally on your device until the App is uninstalled.
  • Information included in support emails will be deleted within a reasonable period after the case is resolved.
  • You may export/import your settings as files. These files remain under your control (e.g., on your device or the cloud storage you choose). They are not transmitted to or stored by the Developer.

Article 6 (Data Security)

  • App data is stored locally and is not intended to be directly accessed by third parties.
  • Data migration is implemented to help preserve data across updates.
  • External communications are limited to the minimum necessary (e.g., when you send a support email).

Article 7 (User Rights)

  • Delete or modify settings within the App
  • Delete all data by uninstalling the App
  • Export/import settings and choose/manage where such files are saved

Article 8 (Disclaimer)

  1. The App is provided “as is” without warranties of fitness for a particular purpose or uninterrupted operation.
  2. Functionality may be affected by changes in website structures or JavaScript specifications.
  3. The Developer is not responsible for loss or corruption of user-provided data.

Article 9 (Changes to the Policy)

The Developer may update this Policy as necessary. Updates take effect upon publication in the App or on the Developer’s website.
